    5. Inferential statistics is the process of using statistical analyses to draw conclusions about a population based on a sample of data. This enables businesses to make predictions and draw conclusions about their operations, customers, and market dynamics.

    A manufacturing company is looking to improve the efficiency of its production processes. The company collects data on the time it takes to produce a specific product, and then performs a hypothesis test to determine whether the mean production time is statistically different from a target value. Based on the results of the test, the company identifies that the mean production time is higher than the target value, indicating that there may be opportunities to improve the efficiency of the production process.
